About

I am a graduate researcher in Electrical Engineering specializing in wireless communication systems. My research focuses on MIMO-OFDM systems, power-domain NOMA, and advanced receiver design techniques such as successive interference cancellation (SIC) and channel estimation.

My work involves mathematical modeling, simulation (MATLAB), and performance evaluation using metrics such as BER vs SNR under realistic channel conditions.

Research

MIMO-OFDM System Modeling

Developed a complete matrix-based MIMO-OFDM framework with N transmit antennas and M subcarriers. The system includes:

  • IFFT/FFT-based OFDM modulation
  • Frequency-selective channel modeling
  • Linear detection techniques

Key Contribution: End-to-end mathematical formulation of transmitter, channel, and receiver.

PD-NOMA with SIC (Uplink)

Implemented a power-domain NOMA system supporting multiple users transmitting simultaneously. Successive interference cancellation (SIC) is applied at the receiver.

  • Power allocation strategies
  • SIC decoding order
  • Performance comparison with OMA

Key Result: Improved spectral efficiency compared to OMA systems.

SIMO NOMA Receiver Design

Designed a SIMO-based NOMA system where multiple receive antennas are jointly used to improve detection reliability.

  • Joint signal combining
  • Channel estimation (LS)
  • Diversity gain analysis
